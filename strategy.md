# Strategy Architecture

## Overview

This is a **compression breakout momentum strategy** built on the thesis that the best risk-adjusted entries occur when:
1. A stock has been compressing (low volatility, narrowing Bollinger Bands)
2. It is outperforming the market (positive RS vs SPY)
3. It breaks out with expanding volume (RVOL > 1.5x) and momentum (RSI rising, EMA9 slope positive)

The edge comes from **right-tail convexity** — the occasional 3-5R winner that runs. The entire system is designed to find those setups and then stay out of the way while they develop.

---

## Signal Hierarchy (Entry)

Signals are generated by `strategies/momentum.py` (`SignalHierarchy`) in four tiers:

### Tier 1 — Market Regime
- EMA9 > EMA21 > EMA50 (bullish stack)
- Price above EMA200
- RSI > 40
- Fails → NO_TRADE (no entry regardless of individual stock quality)

### Tier 2 — Setup Quality
- BB width percentile ≤ 50 (compression)
- RS vs SPY 20-bar ≥ 0 (outperforming)
- RVOL ≥ 1.1 (participation)
- RSI 45–80 (momentum zone, not overbought)

### Tier 3 — Trigger
- EMA9 cross with slope rising
- Price above recent swing high (breakout confirmation)
- ATR expansion (volatility expanding from compression)

### Tier 4 — Risk/Reward
- R:R ≥ 2:1
- Stop below structural low (ATR-based)
- Target at next resistance or 2R

## Position Review Architecture (Two-Phase)

Position management is handled by `strategies/position_manager.py` (`PositionReviewer`).

The reviewer is **state-aware** — it tracks where each trade is in its lifecycle and applies different logic depending on the phase. This aligns the reviewer with the strategy's source of alpha: **trend persistence and right-tail convexity**.

### The Core Design Principle

> The reviewer's job is to kill failed breakouts quickly, then get out of the way.

Most failed breakouts fail within the first 3-5 bars. After that, if the trade is working, the reviewer should become intentionally permissive — normal pullbacks, RVOL normalization, and EMA9 flattening are characteristics of trend development, not failure signals.

### Phase 1 — Validation

**Duration:** From entry until any transition condition is met.

**Goal:** Kill failed breakouts quickly. Protect capital aggressively.

**Exits allowed:**
- Regime flip to NO_TRADE
- Price crosses back below EMA9 AND EMA9 slope is falling (trigger invalidated)
- Catastrophic RVOL collapse (< 0.5x) — breakout participation evaporated
- RAISE_TARGET if price reaches original target
- RSI divergence → PARTIAL_PROFIT (trail stop)
- RVOL fading (< 0.8x) → TRAIL_STOP
- EMA9 slope flattening → TRAIL_STOP

**Transition to Phase 2 when ANY of:**
1. MFE ≥ 1R — trade has reached 1× initial risk in profit (true MFE, not current unrealized)
2. 3 consecutive closes above EMA9 with slope rising — structural confirmation
3. Close > entry_price + 1.0× ATR — price has moved away from the breakout level

### Phase 2 — Participation

**Duration:** After transition, for the life of the trade.

**Goal:** Let the trend run. Preserve convexity. Avoid micromanagement.

**Exits allowed:**
- Regime flip to NO_TRADE
- Close < EMA21 AND RS vs SPY 10-bar ≤ -5% (structural breakdown — both conditions required)
- RAISE_TARGET if price reaches original target (extend winners)

**Suppressed (intentionally):**
- RVOL fading — normal post-breakout normalization
- EMA9 slope flattening — normal in trending markets
- RSI divergence — noise in a trend
- EMA9 single-candle crosses — noise

### Why Both Conditions for Phase 2 Exit

The dual-condition exit (EMA21 AND RS < -5%) avoids killing normal pullbacks. Neither condition alone is sufficient:
- Price below EMA21 alone: happens on every healthy pullback
- RS < -5% alone: can happen during sector rotation while the stock is still trending

Both together = structural breakdown. The stock is losing price structure AND underperforming the market simultaneously.

### State Tracking

Per-position state is persisted in `data/bot_state.json` under `position_state[SYMBOL]`:

```json
{
  "entry_price": 150.00,
  "breakout_level": 148.50,
  "initial_stop_price": 144.00,
  "initial_risk": 6.00,
  "bars_since_entry": 7,
  "max_price_since_entry": 158.20,
  "min_price_since_entry": 149.10,
  "phase": "participation"
}
```

**Key fields:**
- `initial_risk` = `abs(entry_price - initial_stop_price)` — never changes, used for MFE in R units
- `max_price_since_entry` / `min_price_since_entry` — true MFE tracking (not current unrealized)
- `breakout_level` — actual breakout price before ATR buffer (stored at order submission)
- `phase` — once set to `participation`, never reverts

**State cleanup:** When a position closes (stop hit, target hit, or manual close), its state is removed from `bot_state.json`. This prevents stale state from contaminating a future trade in the same symbol.

**Phase transition logging:**
```
NVDA: validation → participation | reason=MFE >= 1R (1.23R)
```

## Backtest Engine

`backtester/engine.py` (`BacktestEngine`) runs a walk-forward backtest with strict no-lookahead.

**Two modes:**
1. **Bracket-only** (`use_position_review=False`): Hard stop/target brackets only. Baseline.
2. **Reviewer mode** (`use_position_review=True`): PositionReviewer runs bar-by-bar after entry, using the same two-phase logic as live trading.

**Comparing the two modes** is the primary way to measure whether the reviewer adds or destroys value:
- If reviewer mode has higher expectancy and similar or better total R → reviewer is adding value
- If reviewer mode has lower total R but better Sharpe → reviewer is smoothing at the cost of convexity
- If reviewer mode has lower total R AND lower Sharpe → reviewer is destroying value

**Key metrics to compare:**
- `expectancy` (R per trade)
- `total_r` (cumulative R)
- `max_drawdown_r` (max drawdown in R units)
- `avg_win_r` vs `avg_loss_r` (distribution shape)
- Average `bars_held` on winning trades (reviewer should not be cutting winners short)

## Automation

The autonomous bot (`strategies/auto_manager.py`) runs on a scheduler (`scheduler.py`):

- **Position review:** Every hour during market hours (equity) or 24/7 (crypto)
- **Entry scan:** Every 30 minutes during market hours
- **Stale order cancellation:** 3:45 PM ET (15 min before close)
- **Daily equity snapshot:** 9:30 AM ET (market open)

Circuit breakers:
- Manual halt via dashboard
- Max daily loss % (configurable, default 2%)

All bot actions are logged to `data/bot_actions.json` and visible in the Bot page.

## Design Philosophy

> "The biggest edge in momentum is the occasional massive trend. Most systems accidentally destroy that edge trying to optimize."

The system is biased toward **convexity over smoothness**:
- Wide initial stops (structural, ATR-based)
- Aggressive early protection (validation phase)
- Intentionally permissive once confirmed (participation phase)
- Minimal interference with working trades

The reviewer is not trying to maximize win rate or minimize drawdown. It is trying to:
1. Kill failed breakouts before they become full stops
2. Stay out of the way of working trades
3. Extend winners when momentum confirms (RAISE_TARGET)
