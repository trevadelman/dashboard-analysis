Satellite  Brand Story & Design System

------------------------------
The Core Idea

Satellite is a precision trading system built to remove the emotional noise
from investing. The name implies distance  watching from above, seeing the
full picture, unaffected by the turbulence happening at ground level. The
brand's entire job is to make that feeling tangible: calm, confident, and
in control.

Most trading tools look like trading tools  dark terminals, aggressive
reds and blues, data-dense interfaces designed to create urgency. Satellite
deliberately goes the other direction. The interface feels like a wellness
app with a trading engine underneath. That is not accidental. That is the
whole point.


------------------------------
Brand Rationale

   - *The problem with fintech aesthetics* is that they are designed to
   make you feel the market. Every red flash, every urgent orange badge, every
   dark terminal surface is optimized for attention and anxiety. Satellite
   rejects this entirely.
   - *The emotional goal* is how you feel *after* a good workout  not
   during. Effort has already happened (the algorithm did it). What you see is
   the outcome. Calm. Green. Up.
   - *Automated, systematic trading should feel boring in the best possible
   way.* You set the rules. The machine runs them. You check in and
   everything is working. That feeling  quiet confidence  is what every
   color, surface, and interaction is designed to deliver.
   - *Green is not just a color here. It is a report card.* When positions
   are doing what they are supposed to do  going up  the entire interface
   reflects that. The brand color and the win color are the same family. The
   app looks healthy when the portfolio is healthy. That is intentional.
   - *Wellness is the emotional layer over a completely systematic core.*
   The mint accent, the light surfaces, the restrained use of red  all of it
   says *we have this handled.* You do not need to feel the volatility. The
   algorithm already priced it in.


------------------------------
The Mark  Easter Eggs & Hidden Meaning

   - *The S is a $*  the vertical wick line passes through the center of
   the S-form, completing the dollar sign. Not a logo that happens to be about
   money. A mark that *is* money, disguised as a letter.
   - *The S is a candlestick*  the same vertical line is the wick of a
   candlestick bar. The body of the S is the candle body. The mark literally
   illustrates the mechanism the software is built on.
   - *The  in the wordmark*  the triangle replacing the A in SATELLITE is
   the universal symbol for *up.* Stock is up. Position is up. Things are
   going the right direction. Hidden in plain sight inside the name itself.
   - *The mark is a satellite before it is anything else.* The overall
   geometric form  a central vertical axis with angular panels extending
   outward from the midpoint  is the silhouette of an actual satellite in
   orbit. Solar panels flanking a central body. The wick line is the antenna.
   Everything else the mark communicates (the $, the candle, the arrow) layers
   on top of that primary read. The name and the shape are the same object.
   That is the foundation the other meanings are built on.
   - *Three easter eggs, zero explanation needed.* Each one rewards a
   second look without requiring it. Someone who doesn't know reads SATELLITE.
   Someone who does sees the $, the candle, and the arrow in one mark.


------------------------------
Color System Philosophy

The palette is built around forest greens  the color of growth, of nature,
of things that compound quietly over time. This is not the electric blue of
crypto dashboards or the blood-red urgency of trading terminals. It is the
color of a portfolio that is doing what it is supposed to do.

Red exists in this system but it is *reserved strictly for loss.* It never
appears in navigation, branding, buttons, or UI chrome. When you see red in
Satellite, something needs your attention. That restraint is what makes red
meaningful.

------------------------------
Typography Note

The wordmark uses a wide-tracked geometric all-caps sans-serif in the
Futura family. Closest free equivalents: *Jost* or *Montserrat* (Google
Fonts). The tracking should feel like a precision instrument label not a
consumer brand, not a startup. Measured. Deliberate.

------------------------------
Design Principles Summary

   - *Green is always good.* When the interface looks healthy, the
   portfolio is healthy. The two should feel inseparable.
   - *Red means something.* Never use red for decoration. Reserve it
   entirely for loss events so that when it appears, the user takes notice.
   - *Calm over urgency.* Every default state should feel settled. Alerts
   and warnings exist, but they are the exception, never the baseline mood.
   - *The algorithm carries the anxiety.* The user should feel like they
   are checking in on something that is working, not monitoring something that
   might fail.
   - *Restraint earns trust.* A system that does not shout at you is a
   system that is confident in itself.
