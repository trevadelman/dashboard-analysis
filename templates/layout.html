App Guide

Overview Scanner Analysis Market Pulse Autonomous Bot Positions Backtest Watchlist

What is this app?

A self-hosted trading dashboard that connects to your Alpaca brokerage account. It has two modes that work independently or together:

  • Manual mode — use the Analysis Dashboard and Scanner to find setups, then place orders yourself via the trade modal.
  • Autonomous mode — the bot scans the market on a schedule, finds signals, and places bracket orders automatically.

Pages at a glance

Analysis DashboardChart + multi-timeframe analysis for a single symbol. Review and execute trades via a modal.
Market ScannerBatch scan a universe of symbols. Load cached results or run a fresh scan.
Market PulseAggregated view of the latest scan cache — stance, signals, top setups, grade distribution, and AI commentary across all timeframes.
PositionsOpen positions with mini charts, position review verdicts, order adjustments, bot status, action log, and trade history.
BacktestRun the momentum strategy against historical data for any symbol. Results in R-multiples.

First-time setup

  • Open Settings → Alpaca and add your API key + secret. Use paper trading to start.
  • Optionally configure an AI provider (Ollama or DeepSeek) in Settings → AI for AI-assisted signal scoring.
  • Set your risk % and max positions in Settings → App.
  • Run a scan from the Market Scanner — this populates the cache that Market Pulse reads from.

How the scan works

Every symbol is evaluated in two tiers. A symbol must pass Tier 1 before Tier 2 is run.

Tier 1 — RegimeIs the stock in a bullish or bearish trend? Uses EMA stack (9/21/50) and price structure. Bearish stocks are excluded from BUY signals.
Tier 2 — Setup qualityCompression (Bollinger Band width percentile), Relative Strength vs SPY, and Relative Volume. All three must meet thresholds for a signal.

Universes

All Sectors (~500)Static curated list of ~500 liquid GICS stocks. Always available, no cache needed.
Full UniverseCached Alpaca equity universe (~2,000+ symbols). Requires a "Refresh from Alpaca" first. Equity only — no crypto.
All Crypto (Alpaca)All USD-quoted crypto pairs tradeable on Alpaca (~25 pairs).
Custom ListComma-separated symbols you type in. Useful for watchlists.

Result columns

RegimeBULLISH / BEARISH / NEUTRAL based on EMA stack.
TierHow far the symbol got (1 = regime only, 2 = full setup scored).
SignalBUY, SELL, or NONE. Only Tier 2 symbols get a signal.
Score / Grade0–100 composite score. A = 80+, B = 65+, C = 50+, D = below 50.
Rel StrengthPrice return vs SPY over the lookback period. Positive = outperforming.
RVOLRelative volume — today's volume vs the 20-day average. >1.5 = elevated.
BB Width %Bollinger Band width as a % of price. Low = compressed = potential breakout.
RSI14-period RSI at the time of the scan.

Sorting & filtering

Click any column header to sort results by that column. Type in the filter box to instantly narrow by symbol, regime, signal, or grade. Examples: BUY, BULLISH, NVDA, A.

Export

After a scan completes, an Export CSV button appears. Downloads all result rows as a CSV file.

Cache

Every completed scan is saved to a local cache. The autonomous bot also writes to this cache on its scheduled runs. Click Load Cache (via the Cache popover in the header) to view the latest results without re-scanning. Market Pulse reads from this same cache.

What is Market Pulse?

Market Pulse is the highest-level view in the app. It reads from the latest scan cache and aggregates everything into one page — so you can see the state of the entire market at a glance without running a new scan. It's the first place to look each morning.

If no cache exists yet, the page shows a warning with a link to run a scan. The autonomous bot also writes to this cache on its scheduled runs, so Market Pulse stays current automatically when the bot is active.

Cross-timeframe overview cards

Three cards at the top — one per timeframe (Long/Swing/Short). Each shows:

  • Stance badge — the overall market posture for that timeframe (see Stance values below).
  • Regime bar — a horizontal bar showing the % of scanned symbols that are BULLISH vs BEARISH vs NEUTRAL.
  • Avg Score — average Tier 2 composite score across all scanned symbols.
  • Signals — count of active BUY/SELL signals in this timeframe.
  • Setups — count of symbols that are forming (Tier 2 scored but below signal threshold).
  • Total symbols scanned — shown at the bottom of each card.

Stance values

RISK ONStrong bullish majority, multiple signals firing. Favorable conditions for new entries.
SELECTIVEMixed conditions. Some signals present but breadth is not broad. Be selective.
COILINGLow volatility, many setups forming but few signals. Market may be compressing before a move.
WAITWeak breadth, few signals. Not a good environment for new entries.
RISK OFFBearish majority. Avoid new long entries; consider reducing exposure.

Active signals table

All BUY and SELL signals across all timeframes in one table. Columns: Symbol, Timeframe, Signal, Score, Grade, RS vs SPY, RVOL, BB Width%, RSI, Price. Click any row to open that symbol in the Analysis Dashboard.

Top setups by timeframe

Tabbed view (Long / Swing / Short) showing the top 10 highest-scoring symbols for each timeframe — whether or not they have an active signal. Useful for finding symbols that are close to triggering. Same columns as the signals table, plus Regime.

Timeframe report cards

Collapsed accordion section. Expand to see a detailed breakdown per timeframe:

  • Grade distribution — bar chart showing how many symbols scored A / B / C / D.
  • Why signals aren't triggering — the top 5 failure gates (e.g. "Low RVOL", "Bearish regime") and how many symbols failed each one. This is the most diagnostic section — it tells you exactly what the market is missing for signals to fire.

AI market commentary

Collapsed section at the bottom. Click to expand and stream a cross-timeframe AI narrative based on the scan data. Requires an AI provider configured in Settings. Only loads once per page visit.

Cache age

The subtitle under "Market Pulse" shows when the cache was last written and how long ago that was. If the cache is stale, click Run Scan in the header to refresh it.

Symbol lookup

Type any ticker in the search box and click Analyze. The dashboard fetches OHLCV bars and runs the full multi-timeframe analysis. Crypto symbols use slash format (e.g. BTC/USD). You can also navigate here from the Scanner or Market Pulse by clicking any symbol row — the symbol is pre-filled and analysis runs automatically.

Chart timeframe selector

The chart interval selector (1D / 1H / 15m / 5m / 1m) controls what the candlestick chart displays. The analysis always runs all three timeframes (Long/Swing/Short) regardless of the chart interval.

Multi-timeframe analysis

Results stream in as three side-by-side columns — Long (Daily), Swing (Hourly), Short (15-min). Each column shows the Tier 1 and Tier 2 verdict cards as they complete, plus an overall alignment badge (ALIGNED / PARTIAL / CAUTION / MIXED).

Long (Daily)1 year of daily bars. Best for swing-to-position trades held days to weeks.
Swing (Hourly)3 months of hourly bars. Best for multi-day swing trades.
Short (15-min)1 month of 15-minute bars. Best for intraday and short-term trades.

Chart indicators

Price + EMAsCandlestick chart with 9/21/50 EMA overlays. EMA stack alignment = trend direction.
RSI14-period RSI. Above 55 = bullish momentum, below 45 = bearish.
MACD12/26/9 MACD. Histogram color shows momentum direction.
Bollinger Bands20-period BB. Narrow bands = compression = potential breakout setup.
VolumeBar volume with 20-day average overlay. Spikes confirm moves.

Signal cards

When a signal is found, a card appears above the timeframe columns showing: BUY/SELL badge, entry/stop/target levels, R:R ratio, and an AI confidence bar (if AI is configured). Each signal card has a Review & Execute button.

Trade modal

Clicking Review & Execute opens a modal pre-filled with the signal's entry, stop, and target. Position size is estimated at 1% of your account equity divided by the stop distance. You can choose GTC (Good Till Cancelled) or Day order duration. The order is submitted as a bracket order — entry limit + stop loss + take profit in one.

AI commentary

If an AI provider is configured, a consolidated commentary card streams in below the timeframe columns after analysis completes. Each timeframe column also gets its own per-timeframe commentary inline.

Decision flow

The bot runs on a fixed schedule. Every job follows this sequence:

  • Circuit breakers — if manually halted or daily loss limit breached, stop immediately.
  • Exit detection — compare current positions to last known; log any that disappeared (stop/target hit).
  • Position review — for each open position, run the reviewer and trail the stop or force-exit if warranted.
  • Entry scan — scan the watchlist; submit bracket orders for new signals that pass all gates.

Schedule (equity watchlists)

9:30 ET Mon–FriSnapshot opening equity, reset daily halt.
9:35 ET Mon–FriReview long (daily) positions.
9:40 ET Mon–FriScan for long signals.
Every 5 minExit detection poller (market hours).
Every 15 minReview short (15-min) positions + scan for short signals.
Every 60 minReview swing positions + scan for swing signals.

Schedule (crypto watchlist)

When the watchlist is set to All Crypto (Alpaca), the bot switches to a 24/7 schedule. The three daily jobs fire at midnight ET every day (including weekends) instead of 9:30 ET Mon–Fri. Interval jobs (15-min, hourly, exit detection) run around the clock.

Settings

Enable autonomousMaster switch. Off = bot scans but never places orders.
Scan WatchlistUniverse the bot scans. Choosing crypto also switches the schedule to 24/7 mode.
Max Daily Loss %If account equity drops this % from the opening snapshot, the bot halts for the rest of the day.
Entry CooldownMinimum hours between bot entries on the same symbol. Prevents re-entering a position too soon.
Review TimeframesWhich timeframes the position reviewer runs on. Match to the timeframes you trade.

Orders

The bot always submits bracket orders — a limit entry with a stop loss and take profit baked in. If the bot goes offline, your stops remain active on Alpaca's servers.

Circuit breaker

Triggered automatically when the daily loss limit is breached, or manually via the Pause button on the Positions page. Resets at the next market open (or midnight for crypto).

Per-position blacklist

Each position card has a Bot Managed toggle immediately to the right of the "Position Review" heading. When toggled off, the bot skips that symbol entirely — it will not trail the stop, force an exit, or enter a new position on that symbol. The blacklist persists across server restarts. Use this when you want to manage a position manually without interference from the bot's automated review loop.

Monitoring

All bot activity is visible on the Positions page — the Autonomous Bot section shows running/halted status, scheduled job next-run times, and the last 20 actions with timestamps.

Summary bar

The top of the page shows total open positions count, combined market value, and total unrealized P&L across all positions.

Position cards

Each open position is an expandable accordion card. Collapsed view shows symbol, quantity, current price, and unrealized P&L. Expanded view has two panels:

  • Left — Position Summary: stats grid (Avg Entry, Current Price, Market Value, Unrealized P&L), a 3-month daily candlestick mini-chart with entry/stop/target price lines drawn on it, and a price level legend.
  • Right — Position Review: auto-runs when the card opens. Shows a verdict, momentum health grid, suggested adjustments, and action buttons.

Position review verdicts

HOLDMomentum is intact. No action needed.
TRAIL STOPPrice has moved favorably. Suggested new stop level shown.
RAISE TARGETStrong momentum. Suggested new target level shown.
PARTIAL PROFITConsider taking partial profits at current levels.
EXITMomentum has deteriorated. Consider closing the position.

Momentum health grid

Six indicators shown per position: Regime (BULLISH/BEARISH), RSI + trend direction, EMA9 slope %, Relative Volume, Extension (ATR multiples from EMA9), and price vs EMA9 (ABOVE/BELOW).

Adjust orders

Pre-filled inputs for stop and target prices (populated with suggested values from the review). Click Apply Adjustment to modify the live bracket order on Alpaca. Click Close Position to submit a market close order (with inline confirmation).

Bot Managed toggle

Each position card has a Bot Managed toggle immediately to the right of the "Position Review" heading. When toggled on (blue), the autonomous bot will trail stops, force exits, and consider new entries on that symbol as normal. When toggled off, the bot skips that symbol entirely — useful when you want to manage a position manually without the bot interfering. The state persists across server restarts. A robot icon in the collapsed card header also reflects the current state (red outline = blacklisted).

Autonomous Bot section

Shows the bot's running/halted/auto-entry status, today's action count, daily open equity snapshot, and a table of scheduled jobs with their next run times. Use the Pause / Resume button to manually halt or resume the bot.

Recent Bot Actions

A log of the last 20 bot actions: Entry, Trail Stop, Exit Detected, Circuit Breaker, etc. — with timestamp, action type, symbol, and result summary.

Trade History

All entries and exits logged by the app (both manual and bot-placed). Shows symbol, side, timeframe, date, quantity, entry/stop/target/exit prices, P&L, and status (Open / Stop Hit / Target Hit / Manual close).

What it tests

Runs the same momentum strategy (Tier 1 + Tier 2) against historical OHLCV data for a single symbol. Simulates entries and exits bar-by-bar using the same signal logic the live bot uses. Results are expressed in R-multiples (units of initial risk), not dollar amounts.

Parameters

SymbolAny equity or crypto symbol.
TimeframeLong (daily), Swing (hourly), or Short (15-min).
PeriodHistorical lookback: 3 months, 6 months, 1 year, 2 years, or 3 years.

Summary stats

TradesTotal trades taken, broken down as Wins / Losses / still Open at end of period.
Win Rate% of closed trades that were profitable. Also shows avg win R and avg loss R.
ExpectancyAverage R per trade. Positive = the strategy has edge over this period.
Total RCumulative R earned across all trades. The equity curve plots this over time.
Max DrawdownLargest peak-to-trough drop in cumulative R during the test.

Equity curve

Plots cumulative R over time (not dollar equity). The zero line is shown as a dashed reference. A rising curve = positive expectancy over the period.

Trades table

Every simulated trade with entry date, action (BUY/SELL), entry/stop/target prices, outcome (win/loss/open), R multiple, exit date, bars held, and tier. Exportable as CSV.

Limitations

  • No slippage or commission modelling — live results will differ.
  • Fills assume the next bar's open price after a signal bar.
  • Only tests one symbol at a time — portfolio-level backtesting is not supported.

What is the Watchlist?

The Watchlist is a personal tracking list for setups you want to monitor over time. When you run analysis on a symbol and want to keep an eye on it, click the bookmark icon (top-right of the Analysis Dashboard) to snapshot the setup and add it to the list.

Adding a symbol

After running a multi-timeframe analysis, a bookmark button appears in the dashboard header. Clicking it saves a snapshot of the current setup — price, scores across all three timeframes, best grade, and signal — at that exact moment. Each symbol can only appear once on the list.

Snapshot columns (frozen at add time)

Price @ AddThe last bar's close price when you added the symbol.
Score L/S/15mComposite scores for Long (Daily) / Swing (Hourly) / Short (15-min) timeframes at the time of adding. Format: 72/58/41. A dash means no scan data was available for that timeframe.
GradeBest grade across all three timeframes at add time. A = 80+, B = 60+, C = 40+, D = below 40.
SignalBUY or SELL if any timeframe had an active signal when you added it; NONE otherwise.

Current columns (from scan cache)

These columns update whenever you run a scan. They reflect the latest scan cache values — not live prices.

Current PricePrice from the most recent scan (Long timeframe preferred).
Δ%Price change from when you added the symbol to the current cached price. Green = up, red = down.
Score L/S/15mCurrent slash-lined scores from the latest scan cache across all three timeframes.
GradeBest current grade across all three timeframes.
SignalCurrent signal from the latest scan. BUY/SELL if any timeframe is triggering.

Notes

Each row has an inline notes field. Click it to type, then press Enter or click away to save. Notes persist across sessions.

Navigating to analysis

Click any symbol in the list to open it in the Analysis Dashboard with the symbol pre-filled and analysis auto-running.

Refreshing current data

Click the Refresh button at the top of the page to reload the watchlist and pull the latest values from the scan cache. To get truly fresh data, run a new scan from the Market Scanner first.

Removing a symbol

Click the trash icon at the end of any row to remove it from the watchlist. This cannot be undone.
